Guide to Employee Bonus Evaluations
Fair & Objective Evaluation: To ensure fairness, base your evaluations on observed behaviors and measurable results, not personal feelings or relationships.
The Evaluation Process
  1. Review each question carefully before scoring
  2. Consider the employee's performance over the entire evaluation period
  3. Score each question within the specified range
  4. Provide additional comments for scores that are exceptionally high or low
  5. Submit the evaluation once all questions are completed
Understanding the Scoring Ranges
Range Interpretation Example
Negative values
(-10 to -1)		 Performance issues that actively detract from team/company goals.
  • -1 to -3: Minor issues
  • -4 to -7: Significant problems
  • -8 to -10: Critical deficiencies
 For "Meeting Deadlines" (-5): Employee consistently misses deadlines, causing project delays
Zero (0) Neutral performance - neither adding value nor causing problems. Often represents a baseline minimal expectation. For "Team Collaboration" (0): Employee does their part but doesn't actively collaborate beyond what's required
Low positive
(1 to 3)		 Performance that meets basic expectations with occasional strengths. For "Quality of Work" (2): Work meets standards but rarely exceeds them
Medium positive
(4 to 7)		 Solid performance that consistently meets and sometimes exceeds expectations. For "Problem Solving" (6): Employee effectively addresses most problems and occasionally finds innovative solutions
High positive
(8 to 10)		 Exceptional performance that consistently exceeds expectations.
  • 8: Excellent performance
  • 9: Outstanding performance
  • 10: Extraordinary performance (rare)
 For "Initiative" (9): Employee consistently identifies improvement opportunities and implements solutions without being asked
Avoiding Common Biases
  • Recency Bias: Focus on the entire evaluation period, not just recent events
  • Halo/Horn Effect: Evaluate each question independently, don't let one attribute influence others
  • Central Tendency: Use the full range of scores when appropriate, not just middle values
  • Leniency/Severity: Apply consistent standards across all employees
Save vs. Submit
  • Save: Stores your current progress but allows further editing later
  • Submit: Finalizes the evaluation and sends it to HR for review
Important: Once submitted, evaluations can only be modified if rejected by HR.
